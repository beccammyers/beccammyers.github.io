5a. Accesses, Uses, and Maintains Health and Safety Records

Document Description: At the beginning of preschool lab, the other teachers and I gathered different health and safety records, such as proof of immunizations, insurance information, and consent forms for each of the children in the lab. These documents are required for each child to participate in lab and helped us ensure that we had the proper legal documentation for their participation and to help keep everyone safe. This document is a template of the one we used to record what documents we had for each child and demonstrates my understanding of how important it is to have a list of health and safety information.

Document Description: While in my family and community relationships class, I learned how to properly store confidential items, such as health and safety information and educational records, behind at least two locks. While in toddler and preschool lab, I practiced this as we kept a bin of health records and other child information behind two locked doors in our classroom. Each day in lab, I had to make sure the records were never left alone in the open, and that when I left for the day, they were locked in the closet with the classroom door locked. This shows that I understand the laws that are in effect to protect people’s privacy.

5b. Responsive to Policy and Procedures to Prevent & Respond to Health, Safety, & Nutrition

Document Description: While in toddler and preschool lab, I read the BYU-Idaho Child Labs Student Handbook. I implemented the policies and procedures outlined in the handbook while in both labs. Some of these procedures included diapering and other safety procedures, expectations for student professionalism, and confidentiality policies. I learned that it is important to have procedures and policies in place not only to protect the children I worked with, but myself and the other teachers in my lab.

5c. Training and Collaboration of Staff

Document Description: Before working with children in the preschool and toddler labs, I received special training on first aid and CPR. I learned how to respond to a variety of emergency situations, including simple cuts and bruises along with more severe situations, such as shock or lacerations. Luckily, I didn’t have to use this training for anything more than bruises and simple scrapes, but I have the training should I ever need to use it.

5d. Connects Families with Community Resources

Document Description: During my Intervention with Infants class, I wrote a resource plan with a partner. The parents I worked with expressed their concerns, and then my partner and I created a list of resources available to help them address the concerns. During an intervention session, we explained the resources we found. I learned that it can sometimes be a daunting task to sift through all the information available online, especially for parents who don’t know where to start looking for ideas to help their child. That’s why it’s important for professionals to help parents by giving them a few resources that they can use to help find more.

5e. Individualized and Group Nutritional, Physical, and Medical Needs

Document Description: One of the children in my toddler lab was very allergic to peanuts. His mother trained us on how to use an Epi-Pen just in case he was to have a reaction during lab. The other teachers and I had to make sure that we didn’t eat anything with peanuts the days we had lab, and we also made sure that the other parents were aware and could make sure their children didn’t come into lab with peanut butter on their clothes or bodies. I learned that it is very important to make sure everyone involved is aware of the situation and trained to help in case of an emergency.

Document Description: While in preschool lab, I prepared snacks for the children several times during the semester. I learned the nutritional needs children have and how to appropriately portion servings. This is a picture of the snack schedule. If the items listed for snack that day weren’t available, I learned how to make appropriate substitutions. This shows that I am capable of preparing and serving children nutritional food that is good for their development.

5f. Medical & Other Conditions, Special Needs

Document Description: Over the last three years, I volunteered at the Helping Hands and Life Skills classes held on campus for adults with disabilities. Every week we did crafts and other games or learned basic skills, such as writing letters. I got to know some of the people really well and had a wonderful experience working with adults who have disabilities such as Down syndrome and cerebral palsy. This is a reflection I wrote for my Family and Community Relationships class about my experiences volunteering. This shows that I have experience working with a variety of ages, not just children.

Document Description: While in toddler lab, I worked with a child who was born with the lower part of her leg missing. The occupational therapist trained all of the teachers in my lab on how to help her successfully crawl around the room. In addition, we planned activities and room design so that she could access all the activities with minimal assistance. This experience taught me how to accommodate for physical disabilities and shows that I am willing to make changes to fit the needs of my specific students.

5g. Emergency Procedures, Abuse Prevention and Reporting

Document Description: During my preschool and toddler lab experiences, we learned procedures for emergencies such as fire alarms, earthquakes, and active shooters. We practiced the procedures for fire drills with the children every month so that everyone was familiar with the plan and knew what to do in case there was a real emergency. This shows that I am prepared to learn proper procedures for keeping the children in my care safe.

Document Description: Before working with the children in toddler lab, I took a short course on abuse recognition and prevention called Stewards of Children. I learned signs of emotional and physical abuse and how to gather more information without leading children to give specific answers. I also learned how to properly report it to my superiors and Child Welfare. This is the booklet we used during the training, showing that I am capable of keeping the children I work with safe by recognizing possible signs of abuse.

5h. Infectious/Communicable Disease

Document Description: While in preschool lab, I was trained on how to properly prepare food for snack time. I learned the procedures for washing my hands, preparing food, and cleaning up to avoid contaminating food and dishes. These procedures consisted of wearing a special apron, wearing gloves, and washing dishes properly. This helped me keep myself, the children in my lab, and those in other labs safe. This shows that I understand procedures for working with food and can implement them to keep disease or infections from spreading.

Document Description: While in toddler lab, there was a child who had special needs and would sometimes vomit when nervous or excited. The other teachers in the lab and I always carried certain black cloths that are specifically designated to clean up bodily fluids so there isn’t contamination. Several times in lab, this child vomited, and we put a black cloth covering it to keep the children away while we got our cleaning supplies. I learned that it is important for everyone to understand proper procedures for preventing illnesses and diseases from spreading, because it is easy to contaminate items.
